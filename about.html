Shri Rama — Avatar, King, Teacher

Lord Rama is revered as the seventh avatar of Vishnu and the central figure of the Ramayana. He is celebrated for his unwavering commitment to dharma (righteousness), compassion, humility and devotion.

Birth & Lineage

Born in Ayodhya to King Dasharatha and Queen Kaushalya, Rama belonged to the Ikshvaku solar dynasty.

Exile & Trials

Rama's 14-year exile, Sita's abduction and the battle against Ravana are central episodes that test virtue and resolve.

Key Episodes

Below are some pivotal moments from the Ramayana that shape Rama's legacy.

Exile (Vanvas)

Rama accepts his father's promise and goes into exile, teaching selflessness and obedience to dharma.

Sita's Abduction

Sita's abduction by Ravana triggers a quest for rescue and the building of alliances, including Hanuman's devotion.

Battle with Ravana

Rama's victory over Ravana is upheld as the triumph of justice and moral order.

Teachings & Significance

Rama's life is often studied not just as narrative but as a guide to right action, leadership and compassion.

Dharma

Perform one's duty with integrity, even amidst hardship.

Compassion

Rama's empathy extends to allies and even adversaries where possible.

Leadership

A ruler who values justice, the welfare of subjects and moral example.

Festivals & Observances

Rama Navami (celebrating Rama's birth) and the Ram Leela (dramatic retellings) are major observances. Diwali marks Rama's return to Ayodhya after exile.
